1. Executive summary  
Over the measured period, digital traffic exhibited a modest decline of 8.33% from start to end, with overall conversion holding steady at 2.90%. Total revenue reached £36,050, driven by an average order value (AOV) of £134.51 and revenue per session of £3.90. Conversion rates fluctuated day-to-day, peaking at 3.18% and nadiring at 2.14%, indicating potential volatility in user engagement or external factors.

2. Most commercially important findings  
- Despite declining sessions, revenue remained robust due to a relatively high AOV, underscoring the value of each converted user.  
- Conversion rates showed daily variability of over 1 percentage point, highlighting opportunities to identify and replicate factors driving the best-performing days.  
- Revenue per session suggests room to improve engagement or conversion efficiency, given the low absolute figure versus AOV.

3. Potential hypotheses  
- The drop in sessions may reflect seasonality, marketing spend reductions, or external competitive pressures rather than a decline in user interest or site experience.  
- Higher conversion on peak days could be linked to promotions, website optimizations, or external events influencing purchase behavior.  
- The disparity between high AOV and low revenue per session suggests many sessions do not convert, potentially due to user experience friction or misaligned traffic quality.

4. Recommended follow-up analyses  
- Segment session and conversion data by acquisition channel and campaign to identify sources driving quality traffic versus decline.  
- Correlate conversion fluctuations with marketing activities, promotions, or site changes to understand drivers of peak and trough days.  
- Analyze user journey analytics and funnel drop-off points to uncover barriers to conversion.  
- Monitor session quality metrics (e.g., bounce rate, time on site) to better gauge engagement beyond raw traffic volume.

5. Data limitations  
- The analysis lacks granular segmentation such as traffic sources, device types, and customer demographics, limiting actionable insights.  
- Absence of time-series revenue breakdowns restricts understanding of intra-period trends.  
- Without qualitative data (e.g., survey feedback or usability testing), user intent and experience cannot be directly assessed.  
- Single aggregate conversion and revenue values prevent isolating drivers of performance variation.  

In summary, while total revenue and AOV are encouraging, declining session volumes and fluctuating conversion rates warrant deeper investigation to sustain and grow digital commercial performance.
